🍱 Hokkaido Food Guide — Japan's Food Basket

Hokkaido is widely regarded as Japan's food basket — surrounded by cold northern seas producing the sweetest seafood, and producing over half of Japan's milk. Use the 🌟 Picks tab to add these to your itinerary. Items tagged Food Guide are sourced from culinary research.

🦀 Fresh Seafood

Kaisendon (seafood bowls) with uni, ikura, scallops & crab. Try hairy crab (kegani) for sweet meat and rich roe. Conveyor belt sushi in Hokkaido is world-class — look for hakaku (octagonal fish). Don't miss ichiya-boshi grilled hokke (overnight-dried mackerel).

🍜 Regional Ramen

Miso ramen — Sapporo classic, rich & slightly sweet. Shio (salt) ginger ramen — lighter, warming. Ebisoba — shrimp broth at Chitose Airport. Chicken Pyon — modern creamy chicken with rosemary foam.

🍖 Signature Dishes

Genghis Khan (Jingisukan) — cook lamb on a helmet-shaped grill. Soup Curry — Sapporo original with 'Milk Style' variant using Hokkaido milk. Chikuwapan — bread roll with fish cake & tuna mayo, only in Hokkaido!

🍦 Dairy & Sweets

Hokkaido produces over half of Japan's milk. Try Shiroi Koibito cookies, crab cream croquettes made with local milk, Hangansama rice cakes (Hokkaido Shrine exclusive), and campus ice cream at Hokkaido University.

🎌 Unique Delicacies

Tachikama sashimi — bouncy cod milt kamaboko (Oct-Feb winter delicacy). Seicomart Hot Chef — local convenience store with freshly made fried chicken loved by all Hokkaido locals.

📍 Where to Eat by Area

Sapporo: Nijo Market, Susukino, Ramen Republic. Furano: Farm cafes, cheese factory. Hakodate: Morning Market (open 5AM!), Lucky Pierrot. Everywhere: Seicomart stores along driving routes.

👴 Senior-Friendly Travel Guide — 3 Adults + 2 Seniors

This itinerary is designed for a family group of 5 with 2 senior travelers. Every recommendation considers accessibility, pacing, and comfort.

🚗 Driving & Vehicle

7-seater minivan (Toyota Alphard/HiAce) recommended for 5 pax + luggage. Max ~3 hrs per driving leg. Frequent stops at 道の駅 (roadside stations). Hokkaido roads are wide and well-maintained. Adults can rotate driving duties.

♨️ Onsen & Ryokan

3 onsen ryokan stays included — therapeutic for joints and fatigue. Check water temperature first (40-42°C ideal for seniors). Use handrails at all times. Private baths (貸切風呂) available at most ryokans for family use. Book twin rooms for seniors.

♿ Accessibility

Farm Tomita & Shiroi Koibito Park are flat terrain. Ropeways at Mt Moiwa & Hakodate eliminate climbing. Morning markets have seated restaurants. Most airports and JR stations have elevators. Request wheelchair assistance at airports if needed.

🍽️ Dining for 5

Ryokans include dinner & breakfast for all guests. Reserve tables for 5 at popular restaurants. Markets have seated dining halls. Seicomart Hot Chef along driving routes for easy group meals. Avoid late dinner times (before 7 PM ideal for seniors).

⏰ Pacing & Rest

Built-in rest day at Lake Toya — critical for senior recovery. Limit to 2-3 activities per day. Start mornings at 9-10 AM (not too early). Adults can split up for harder activities while seniors rest at hotel/cafe. Afternoon rest breaks (1-3 PM) recommended.

💊 Health & Emergency

Japan's hospitals are excellent. Carry travel insurance docs for all 5 travelers. Save hotel phone numbers. Emergency: 119 (ambulance). Most Hokkaido hospitals have basic English. Bring a printed list of any medications and allergies in Japanese (ask hotel to help translate).

💰 Budget (5 Pax)

Meals: ~¥15,000-20,000/day for 5. Accommodation: ~¥50,000-80,000/night (2-3 rooms). Rental car: ~¥10,000/day (7-seater). Tolls: ~¥3,000-5,000/day. Activities: ~¥5,000-10,000/day total. Senior discounts available at many attractions (65+).
